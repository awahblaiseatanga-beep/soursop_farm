The Soursop Plant

Understanding Annona muricata: Nature's tropical miracle.

The Soursop Tree

The soursop tree (Annona muricata) is a broadleaf, flowering, evergreen tree native to tropical regions. It thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil, making the highlands of Buea an ideal environment for its growth.

The tree can reach heights of 8 to 10 meters and is known for its glossy, dark green leaves and distinctive yellow-green flowers. At L&N Farms, we nurture our trees organically to ensure they remain healthy and productive for generations.

The Fruit

The soursop fruit is large, heart-shaped, and covered in soft, pliable spines. Its skin is dark green when young, turning slightly yellowish-green as it matures. Inside, the flesh is white, fibrous, and juicy, with a unique flavor profile that combines notes of strawberry, pineapple, and citrus.

Rich in Vitamin C, B vitamins, and dietary fiber, soursop is not only delicious but also highly nutritious. We harvest our fruits only when they are fully mature to ensure the perfect balance of sweetness and tanginess.

The Leaves and Herbal Tea

Soursop leaves have been used for centuries in traditional medicine for their various health benefits. They contain powerful antioxidants and compounds that are believed to support the immune system and promote overall well-being.

At L&N Farms, we carefully select the finest leaves from our organic trees. These leaves are then cleaned and dehydrated at low temperatures to preserve their natural properties, creating a premium base for our soursop herbal tea.
